## Key Takeaways

**1. SOC Work is Investigative**  
Being a SOC analyst isn't just about monitoring dashboards or websites, it's more like detective work. Every alert tells a story, and my job is to figure out if it's a real threat or a false alarm. This requires critical thinking, not just technical knowledge.

**2. Context Matters More Than Alerts**  
A single suspicious login isn't necessarily an incident. But a login from an unusual location, at an unusual time, followed by privilege escalation attempts? That's a pattern. Learning to see the bigger picture by correlating multiple events is crucial.

**3. Speed vs Accuracy Balance**  
SOC analysts need to work quickly (incidents escalate fast) but also accurately (false positives waste everyone's time, false negatives are dangerous). This course taught me to make sure not to cut corners on any investigation.

**4. Documentation is Critical**  
Every action I take during an investigation has to be documented. Future analysts, incident responders, or even legal teams might need my notes.
